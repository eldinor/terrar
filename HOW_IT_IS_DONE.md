# How the terrain is built

The terrain is generated in two layers:

1. `buildTerrain(...)` in `src/builder/buildTerrain.ts` resolves config and calls `buildBuilderWorldData(...)`.
2. `buildBuilderWorldData(...)` in `src/builder/internal/buildWorldData.ts` creates a `ProceduralGenerator`, packs its snapshot, and optionally adds POIs and roads.

## 1. World shape

`src/terrain/ProceduralGenerator.ts` creates the base terrain from deterministic noise derived from the seed:

- continent noise gives the large landmass shape
- a radial falloff keeps the world island-like
- mountain-mask noise decides where mountain ranges can appear
- ridged noise adds mountains
- fbm noise adds hills and small detail

The result is clamped between `baseHeight` and `maxHeight`.

## 2. Erosion and water

If enabled in config:

- thermal erosion softens steep slopes
- depression filling builds drainage directions
- flow accumulation estimates where water collects
- river and lake masks are derived from flow, slope, and elevation
- hydraulic erosion carves channels
- sediment is deposited along rivers and lakes

Those fields are stored in the packed snapshot so later systems can reuse them without regenerating everything.

## What slopes define

Slope is not just visual polish here. It is derived from local height differences and then reused by several systems:

- rivers prefer cells that have enough downhill gradient, so slope helps decide where channels appear
- steep terrain becomes more rocky, while flatter terrain favors grass or dirt in the material blending
- flatter areas are preferred for villages, crossings, and road routing
- moderate rocky slopes and mountain flanks are better candidates for mines
- very steep areas are poor for foliage placement

So in practice, slope helps decide both how the terrain looks and what can be built or placed on it.

## 3. Resources, POIs, and roads

From the same sampled world:

- coal, iron, and copper maps are generated procedurally
- POI placement is handled by `src/terrain/TerrainPoiPlanner.ts`
- roads between POIs are generated by `src/terrain/TerrainRoadPlanner.ts`

The builder returns one `BuiltTerrain` asset containing config, POIs, roads, and the packed terrain snapshot.

## 4. Rendering

At runtime, `src/terrain/TerrainSystem.ts` reconstructs a `ProceduralGenerator` from the packed snapshot and creates chunk objects.

Each chunk:

- samples the terrain at one of the LOD grid sizes (`129`, `65`, `33`, `17`)
- builds mesh data in `src/terrain/TerrainMeshBuilder.ts`
- stores terrain data in vertex channels for shading/debug views
- adds skirts on chunk borders to hide cracks between LODs

Materials then blend terrain textures using height, slope, river/lake, sediment, and resource data.

## In short

Noise defines the landform, erosion and hydrology make it believable, planners place gameplay features, and the renderer turns the cached snapshot into chunked LOD meshes.
